Scale & Corrosion — The Twin Threats to Your Boiler

Pakistan's ground and surface water carries high concentrations of dissolved hardness salts (calcium, magnesium), silica, and iron. When this water enters a steam boiler without proper pre-treatment and chemical conditioning, two destructive processes begin:

Scale Formation

As water is evaporated inside the boiler, dissolved salts concentrate and precipitate on the inner surface of boiler tubes as hard mineral deposits — scale. Scale is a thermal insulator. Even a thin scale layer dramatically reduces heat transfer from the burner to the water, forcing the burner to work harder and consume more fuel to produce the same steam output.

Cost impact of boiler scale: A 1mm scale layer on boiler tubes increases fuel consumption by 2%. A 6mm scale buildup — common in untreated Pakistani boilers — wastes 12% of your fuel bill. On a 5-ton/hr boiler running on furnace oil, this can amount to PKR 8–15 million wasted per year. Beyond fuel, thick scale causes thermal stress, tube bulging, and catastrophic tube failures requiring emergency shutdown and costly tube replacement.

Oxygen Corrosion

Dissolved oxygen in boiler feed water attacks boiler metal through electrochemical pitting corrosion — creating characteristic deep pits in boiler tubes, economisers, and steam lines. This corrosion is invisible until catastrophic — tubes can fail suddenly under operating pressure. Iron oxide corrosion products also contaminate steam, damaging downstream equipment and process quality.

Boiler Water Treatment Chemistry

Effective boiler water treatment addresses four key water quality parameters — hardness, oxygen, pH, and suspended solids — using a coordinated programme of chemical dosing and mechanical controls.

  • Scale Inhibitors — Threshold active agents and polymer dispersants that prevent crystallisation of calcium and magnesium salts on tube surfaces, keeping hardness in suspension for blowdown removal
  • Oxygen Scavengers — Chemical oxygen depletion using sodium sulphite or catalysed hydrazine compounds, removing dissolved oxygen to below detectable levels before water enters the boiler
  • pH Conditioners / Alkalinity Builders — Sodium hydroxide and sodium phosphate blends that maintain boiler water pH at the recommended 10.5–11.5 range — the zone where steel corrosion is minimised
  • Sludge Conditioners — Polymer-based dispersants that keep precipitated hardness salts in a mobile, pumpable sludge form that can be removed by blowdown — preventing hard cake deposits
  • Condensate Corrosion Inhibitors — Filming amines and neutralising amines that protect steam condensate return lines from carbonic acid corrosion

Industrial Air Humidification Systems

Relative humidity (RH) is a critical process parameter in textile manufacturing, paper mills, pharmaceutical production, and food processing. Insufficient humidity causes yarn breakage, increased loom stops, electrostatic discharge, fibre brittleness, and significant production loss. Seamens Enterprises is an authorised principal partner of CONAIR and LUWA-Nederman — the world's leading names in industrial air treatment — for Pakistan.

Why Humidity Control Matters in Textile Mills

  • Spinning departments — Low RH increases yarn hairiness, end-breaks, and machine stops. Target: 55–65% RH
  • Weaving departments — Warp yarn becomes brittle and breaks under low humidity, increasing loom stops by 30–50%. Target: 65–75% RH
  • Sizing departments — Moisture content of sized warp affects adhesion and film quality. Target: 50–60% RH
  • Fabric storage — Uncontrolled humidity causes fabric weight variation and mould growth. Target: 55–65% RH

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What causes scale buildup in steam boilers in Pakistan?
Scale in Pakistan's boilers is primarily caused by high hardness water (calcium and magnesium salts), silica, and iron in the feed water. Without proper chemical treatment, these precipitate on boiler tubes as hard scale — reducing heat transfer efficiency and risking tube failure.
Q: How does scale affect steam boiler fuel consumption?
Scale is one of the most expensive problems in boiler operation. Just 1mm of scale on boiler tubes increases fuel consumption by 2%. A 6mm scale layer means 12% fuel wasted. Proper chemical treatment keeps tubes clean and fuel costs controlled.
Q: What is the optimum relative humidity for textile mills?
Most textile processes require 55–70% RH. Spinning departments need 55–65% RH, weaving 65–75% RH, and sizing preparation areas 50–60% RH. CONAIR and LUWA humidification systems installed by Seamens maintain precise RH control year-round.
Q: What is an oxygen scavenger and why is it used?
Dissolved oxygen in boiler feed water causes corrosion pitting on boiler tubes and steam lines. Oxygen scavengers chemically neutralise dissolved oxygen, protecting boiler metal and extending equipment life significantly.
