Cooling Tower Water Treatment — Why It Cannot Be Ignored

A cooling tower operates by circulating water to absorb heat from industrial processes — the water then cools through evaporation in the tower. This evaporation process concentrates dissolved minerals in the circulating water over time, creating three major problems:

  • Scale deposition — Calcium carbonate, calcium sulphate, and silica deposit as hard scale on heat exchanger surfaces, reducing heat transfer efficiency by 10–40% and increasing energy consumption dramatically
  • Corrosion — Electrochemical corrosion of carbon steel towers, copper heat exchanger tubes, and piping — leading to pitting, leaks, and premature equipment failure
  • Biological growth — Algae, bacteria (including Legionella), and biofilm thrive in warm, open cooling water — blocking distribution nozzles, insulating heat transfer surfaces, and creating health and safety risks

Key fact: A 1mm scale layer on a heat exchanger reduces its efficiency by 20–30%. For a 500 TR chiller, this translates to a compressor working 15–25% harder — increasing electricity consumption by PKR 3–8 million per year on an untreated system.

RO & Membrane Chemicals

RO membrane performance depends critically on the quality of chemical pre-treatment and ongoing membrane maintenance. Seamens supplies a complete range of RO system chemicals:

  • RO Antiscalant — Dosed into feed water ahead of the RO membrane to prevent calcium carbonate, calcium sulphate, barium sulphate, and silica scaling on membrane surfaces. Extends membrane life and maintains design flux rate
  • Acidic CIP Cleaner — For removal of carbonate, iron, and manganese scale from fouled membranes during clean-in-place (CIP) procedures
  • Alkaline CIP Cleaner — For removal of organic fouling, biofilm, and colloidal matter from membranes — restoring membrane permeability after biological fouling
  • Sodium Bisulphite — Dosed for chlorine neutralisation ahead of membranes when chlorinated feed water is used — protecting polyamide membrane from oxidative damage
  • Biocide for RO — Non-oxidising biocide formulated for RO membrane compatibility — controlling biological growth in storage tanks and pre-treatment equipment without membrane damage
  • Membrane Preservation Solution — Long-term storage preservation for idle RO systems preventing biological growth and membrane drying during shutdown periods

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What chemicals are used in cooling tower water treatment in Pakistan?
Cooling tower water treatment in Pakistan requires scale inhibitors (calcium/silica control), corrosion inhibitors (for steel and copper), biocides (algae, bacteria, Legionella control), and pH control chemicals. Seamens Water Safe 1020 covers all these functions in a coordinated programme.
Q: How often should cooling tower water be tested?
Cooling tower water should be tested at minimum weekly for cycles of concentration, pH, conductivity, and biocide residual. Monthly comprehensive testing covers hardness, alkalinity, iron, and microbiological counts. Seamens provides on-site testing as part of our service programme.
Q: What is RO antiscalant and why is it needed?
RO antiscalant is dosed into feed water before the RO membrane to prevent scaling on the membrane surface. Without antiscalant, calcium carbonate, calcium sulphate, and silica precipitate on the membrane, reducing flux rate, increasing operating pressure, and shortening membrane life.
Q: What chiller capacity range does Seamens supply?
Seamens Enterprises supplies industrial chiller systems from 50 TR to 2000 TR, including air-cooled and water-cooled configurations. We handle complete erection, commissioning, and chemical treatment programmes for chiller systems across Pakistan.
Q: What is Zero Liquid Discharge (ZLD) and does Seamens provide it?
Zero Liquid Discharge (ZLD) is a water treatment system that eliminates all liquid effluent discharge by recovering and reusing process water. Seamens supplies ZLD-integrated RO systems for textile mills and industrial plants — helping clients meet environmental regulations while reducing fresh water consumption.
